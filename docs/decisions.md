# Human Decisions

## From promotion to utility

The old homepage relied on a large promotional banner, dense product imagery, decorative backgrounds, and legacy contact widgets. I changed the organizing principle from “show everything” to “help a visitor find the right business path quickly.”

## Information architecture

The final structure uses six clear destinations:

- Home
- Products
- Repair services
- Downloads
- About
- Contact

Product and service details remain separate because they answer different user needs.

## Visual direction

The new visual system uses industrial typography, a restrained graphite and blue palette, a visible grid, and strong spacing. The goal is precision and operational clarity, not decoration.

## Content integrity

Missing company photography, product images, documents, and a confirmed WeChat QR code were not replaced with generated material. The interface was designed to remain complete without pretending those assets existed.

## AI boundaries

AI could propose, implement, compare, and revise. It could not decide that unverified information was true, publish invented business claims, or redefine the project goals without review.

## Stop condition

Iteration stopped after the structure, navigation, content hierarchy, responsive behavior, and major interaction states met the acceptance criteria. Additional decoration was not treated as progress.
